A Guide To Mars

Location and Orbit

  • Mars is the fourth planet from the sun and has an orbital period of 687 Earth days.
  • It is located about 228 million kilometers from the Sun (1.5 AU away) and take light 13 minutes to reach mars.
  • About half the size of Earth, it has a radius of 3,390 kilometers.

About

  • Mars has distinct seasons as its tilt is about that of Earth at 25 degrees. Though the seasons will last much longer and have varied lengths due to its elliptical orbit around the Sun.
  • There are two moons that orbit mars, Phobos and Deimos, that are thought to be captured asteriods.
  • Known for its red hue, from the oxidized iron, Mars is also home to many canyons and even the largest volcano in the solar system. Named Olympus Mons, it is approximately 21 kilometers high and 600 kilometers wide.
