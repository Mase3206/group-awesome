A Guide To Mercury

Location and Orbit

  • Mercury is the first planet in the solar system and closest to the sun. On average it is about 58 million kilometers from the sun. It ranges from being about 47 million kilometers away to being as far as 70 million kilometers from the sun.
  • With a highly elliptical orbit, Mercury has some points in in orbit where it experiences no sunrises or sunsets.
  • Because the axis of rotation is only 2 degrees Mercury does not experience seasons as other planets do.

About

  • Because of its proximity to the sun Mercury has no moons or atmosphere.
  • Its surface is scarred with craters from the bombardment of comets and meteroids. Many craters are named after famous artists, musicians, and authors.
  • Though it has a relatively weak magnetic field, it's closeness to the sun causes its field to interact with the solar winds, resulting in strong magnetic tornadoes that funnel fast solar wind plasma down to the surface.
