A Guide To Sedna

Location and Orbit

  • Sedna is a dwarf planet orbiting beyond the reach of Neptune.
  • At its aphelion, Sedna is about 937 AU from the Sun. Making it have one of the widest known orbits in the solar system.
  • It also has a highly elliptical and narrow orbit, with an eccentricity of .85.

About

  • Sedna has one of the reddest surfaces of the known bodies in the solar system.
  • So far there are no known moons or rings.
  • The surface is a mixture of solid ices of water, methane, and nitrogen.
